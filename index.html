--- layout: default title: Mark Heimann ---

I am a second-year PhD student in computer science at the University of Michigan working in the GEMS Lab under the supervision of Danai Koutra. Specifically, my current research is primarily in matrix approximation techniques for large-scale machine learning problems. I am interested in how these can affect the theoretical performance of classification, regression, and clustering algorithms, and in how they may be used in large-scale applications such as graph mining.

Other significant pursuits of mine include chess, where I am an active master-level player in both the United States Chess Federation and FIDE, and music. I am interested in creating artificial intelligence technology for music analysis and performance, as well as augmenting acoustic instruments and compositions with electronic technology.
